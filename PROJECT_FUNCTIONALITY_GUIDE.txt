GUN DEALER PROJECT FUNCTIONALITY GUIDE
======================================

This file is the clean handoff guide for the current project state.
It explains:
1. What the project does
2. How to use each gameplay system
3. What each script is responsible for
4. Where the key data lives


1. PROJECT SUMMARY
------------------
This is a first-person gun black market / workshop simulator built in Unity.

The player loop is:
- Walk around the workshop in first person
- Use the computer to inspect buyer orders
- Accept or reject jobs
- Pick up weapon parts
- Place parts on the assembly bench
- Assemble the gun
- Load the finished gun into a gun case
- Ship the case on the conveyor
- Earn cash
- Manage daily jobs, heat, inspections, raids, campaign loss, and campaign victory

The project also includes:
- A runtime HUD and interaction prompt system
- A runtime PC interface with Orders and Shop tabs
- A physical shop stock system
- A tutorial system
- Difficulty presets
- A file-based save system
- Forest encounter/combat content
- A small target range


2. PLAYER CONTROLS
------------------
Default keyboard/mouse controls:
- Mouse: Look
- WASD: Move
- Space: Jump
- Left Shift: Sprint
- E: Interact
- G: Drop held item
- Esc: Cancel / close the PC
- Left Click: Fire an assembled gun when holding one
- 1: Open Orders tab while inside the PC
- 2: Open Shop tab while inside the PC
- Tab: Switch tabs while inside the PC
- R: Restart after campaign loss or campaign win

The project can also use Unity Input System actions where available, but it falls back to legacy input if needed.


3. CORE GAMEPLAY SYSTEMS AND HOW TO USE THEM
--------------------------------------------

3.1 Orders
- Walk to the computer and press E.
- Open the Orders tab.
- Review the buyer, gun name, price, risk, timer, and required parts.
- Accept to start the order or reject it to move on.
- Accepting an order:
  - starts the timer
  - raises heat based on order data
  - updates the day/job counters
  - spawns forest scouts based on order risk
  - updates the world blueprint display

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/OrderManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/GunOrder.cs
- Assets/Scripts/ComputerInteract.cs
- Assets/Scripts/PCManager.cs


3.2 Crafting Bench
- Pick up parts from shelves or purchased stock.
- Walk to the correct bench slot and press E to place a part.
- When all required slots are filled, use the red assemble button.
- If the wrong parts are used, the gun still assembles but reward is reduced.
- Orders can require all 4 parts or only a subset, depending on the GunOrder asset.

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/CraftingManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/PartSlot.cs
- Assets/Scripts/Part.cs
- Assets/Scripts/AssembleButtonInteract.cs
- Assets/Scripts/GunOrder.cs


3.3 Gun Assembly Result
- A successful assembly spawns the final gun prefab.
- The player tries to auto-pick it up.
- The assembled gun is marked as PartType.Assembled.
- If the assembly was wrong, the gun gets spread when firing and the payout penalty applies later.

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/CraftingManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/GunWeapon.cs


3.4 Gun Case and Shipping
- Pick up an empty gun case.
- While holding the assembled gun, interact with the case to load the gun into it.
- Carry the loaded case to the conveyor and press E.
- The conveyor moves the case and triggers payout at the end.

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/GunCase.cs
- Assets/Scripts/ConveyorInteract.cs
- Assets/Scripts/ConveyorManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/RewardManager.cs


3.5 Shop Flow
Current shop behavior:
- Open the computer and switch to the Shop tab.
- Use the supplier terminal to order stock into the physical shop area.
- Go to the world shop stock.
- Press E on a stocked item to buy it with cash.
- Purchased items unlock their real interactions and can be picked up normally.

Notes:
- Shop items can unlock on later days.
- The shop is disabled when the campaign is lost or won.

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/ShopManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/ShopItem.cs
- Assets/Scripts/ShopStockItem.cs
- Assets/Scripts/PCManager.cs


3.6 Cash, Rewards, and Penalties
- Shipping a correct gun pays the full adjusted reward.
- Wrong parts reduce payout.
- Overtime reduces payout.
- Difficulty can raise or lower payout.
- A minimum order payout is enforced.

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/RewardManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/TimerManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/CraftingManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/GunOrder.cs
- Assets/Scripts/DifficultyManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/DifficultyPreset.cs


3.7 Heat, Inspection, and Raid System
- Accepting risky work raises heat.
- Wrong builds and late deliveries raise heat further.
- Clean deliveries can reduce heat.
- At warning heat, an inspection is queued.
- At raid heat, a raid is queued.
- Inspection outcome depends on whether the player can afford the bribe.
- A raid takes cash, resets heat to a lower value, and can shut down the day or campaign.

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/HeatManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/RewardManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/CampaignManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/DayManager.cs


3.8 Day Loop
- The game runs in days.
- Each day has a jobs-per-day limit.
- Completing or rejecting jobs advances day resolution.
- When the day ends, heat can cool down overnight.
- A new day starts after the transition delay unless the campaign is closed.

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/DayManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/OrderManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/HeatManager.cs


3.9 Campaign Loss and Campaign Win
- The campaign is lost after too many raid shutdowns.
- The campaign is won when the cash goal is reached.
- When the campaign is closed:
  - no new orders should generate
  - the shop stops trading
  - the day loop stops advancing
  - the player can restart with R

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/CampaignManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/RewardManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/DayManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/OrderManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/ShopManager.cs


3.10 Tutorial
- On a fresh run, the tutorial walks the player through:
  - opening the PC
  - accepting an order
  - picking up a part
  - placing a part
  - assembling a gun
  - delivering a gun
- Tutorial completion is saved.

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/TutorialManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/PlayerInventory.cs
- Assets/Scripts/PartSlot.cs
- Assets/Scripts/CraftingManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/OrderManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/RewardManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/PCManager.cs


3.11 Difficulty Presets
- Difficulty changes:
  - starting cash
  - minimum payout
  - payout multiplier
  - campaign cash win goal
  - inspection and raid thresholds
  - inspection bribe cost
  - max shutdowns
  - jobs per day
- Difficulty is saved and applied on the next restart.
- Resource presets are provided in Assets/Resources/DifficultyPresets.

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/DifficultyManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/DifficultyPreset.cs


3.12 Saving and Persistence
- The project uses JSON files instead of PlayerPrefs.
- Save data is written under Application.persistentDataPath/GunDealer/.
- Saved values include:
  - cash
  - completed orders
  - heat
  - day
  - shutdown count
  - game over state
  - win state
  - difficulty preset name
  - tutorial completion

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/SaveSystem.cs


3.13 Forest Encounter and Combat
- Accepting an order can spawn forest scouts.
- NPC count depends on order risk.
- The system also auto-builds:
  - forest ground
  - perimeter walls
  - trees
  - NPC spawn points
  - a test range with targets
- The assembled gun can fire projectiles.
- Projectiles damage anything implementing IDamageable.

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/ForestEncounterManager.cs
- Assets/Scripts/ForestNpc.cs
- Assets/Scripts/GunWeapon.cs
- Assets/Scripts/GunProjectile.cs
- Assets/Scripts/IDamageable.cs
- Assets/Scripts/RangeTarget.cs


3.14 Free Parts Per Day
- Optional helper system.
- Can spawn configured free parts at the start of each day.
- Useful for balancing early progression or tutorial-friendly setups.

Important scripts:
- Assets/Scripts/DayStartPartSpawner.cs

6. SCRIPT MAP: WHAT EACH SCRIPT DOES
------------------------------------

Player / Interaction
- Assets/Scripts/PlayerController.cs
  First-person movement, look, jump, sprint, interact, drop, cancel, interaction targeting, and prompt updates.
- Assets/Scripts/PlayerInventory.cs
  Holds exactly one item, manages pickup/drop/release, and keeps held items from clipping badly into the world.
- Assets/Scripts/IInteractable.cs
  Base interaction interface used by world objects.
- Assets/Scripts/Part.cs
  Pickup behavior for physical weapon parts.
- Assets/Scripts/PartSlot.cs
  Bench slot logic for placing and removing parts.
- Assets/Scripts/AssembleButtonInteract.cs
  Interactable red bench button that triggers assembly.
- Assets/Scripts/ComputerInteract.cs
  Interactable computer trigger.
- Assets/Scripts/ConveyorInteract.cs
  Interactable conveyor trigger for shipping a loaded gun case.
- Assets/Scripts/GunCase.cs
  Gun case behavior: pickup, load assembled gun, transit state.

Gameplay Managers
- Assets/Scripts/OrderManager.cs
  Generates orders, manages accept/reject/complete flow, order UI, recipe text, and blueprint display.
- Assets/Scripts/CraftingManager.cs
  Validates required slots, checks mismatches, assembles final guns, and tracks bench status.
- Assets/Scripts/RewardManager.cs
  Computes payouts, modifies cash, updates completed job count, and refreshes the HUD.
- Assets/Scripts/TimerManager.cs
  Tracks build timer and overtime.
- Assets/Scripts/DayManager.cs
  Runs daily job pacing, transitions, and day summaries.
- Assets/Scripts/HeatManager.cs
  Runs heat accumulation, inspections, raids, and related alerts.
- Assets/Scripts/CampaignManager.cs
  Tracks campaign shutdowns, loss state, win state, and restart flow.
- Assets/Scripts/PCManager.cs
  Controls the PC UI, tab switching, camera transitions, and input handling while in the computer.
- Assets/Scripts/ShopManager.cs
  Builds the runtime shop UI, orders physical stock to the shop, and completes purchases.
- Assets/Scripts/DeliveryZone.cs
  Legacy / utility delivery spawn zone script. Not the primary current shop flow, but still useful for spawn-slot style deliveries.
- Assets/Scripts/DayStartPartSpawner.cs
  Optional daily free-part spawner.
- Assets/Scripts/TutorialManager.cs
  Step-based onboarding flow for the full gameplay loop.
- Assets/Scripts/DifficultyManager.cs
  Finds and applies active difficulty presets.
- Assets/Scripts/SaveSystem.cs
  Generic JSON-based save/load layer for persistent state.

Combat / Forest
- Assets/Scripts/ForestEncounterManager.cs
  Builds the forest/test-range environment and spawns forest NPCs on order acceptance.
- Assets/Scripts/ForestNpc.cs
  Simple enemy that chases the player, takes damage, and ragdolls on death.
- Assets/Scripts/GunWeapon.cs
  Fire logic for assembled guns, including rate of fire, spread, and projectile spawning.
- Assets/Scripts/GunProjectile.cs
  Projectile movement, hit detection, damage application, and impact force.
- Assets/Scripts/IDamageable.cs
  Interface for anything that can receive projectile damage.
- Assets/Scripts/RangeTarget.cs
  Reactive shooting target used by the testing range.

Data Definitions
- Assets/Scripts/GunOrder.cs
  ScriptableObject definition for buyer jobs.
- Assets/Scripts/ShopItem.cs
  ScriptableObject definition for supplier items.
- Assets/Scripts/DifficultyPreset.cs
  ScriptableObject definition for difficulty tuning values.

UI / Runtime Helpers
- Assets/Scripts/GameplayUIManager.cs
  Creates and controls the runtime overlay: HUD, prompt, crosshair, toasts, and hints.
- Assets/Scripts/GameFeedback.cs
  Simple helper to send gameplay messages to both the console and runtime toast UI.
- Assets/Scripts/SummaryOverlayManager.cs
  Creates and controls large summary popups for day transitions and campaign state.

  Keeps world-space UI facing the camera.

Editor
- Assets/Scripts/Editor/SceneSetupWizard.cs
  Editor-only helper window that scaffolds common scene objects and manager objects.


7. CURRENT EXPECTED GAMEPLAY FLOW
---------------------------------
1. Start the scene
2. The bootstrap ensures core managers exist
3. The tutorial may start if not completed before
4. The day system begins day 1
5. The order system offers a job
6. The player uses the PC to accept the job
7. The player gathers parts and assembles the gun
8. The player cases the gun and ships it
9. The reward, heat, campaign, and day systems all update
10. New jobs continue until the day ends, the campaign is lost, or the cash goal is reached


8. IMPORTANT CURRENT NOTES
--------------------------
- The current shop flow is "order to physical shop, then buy in world". It is not the old "spawn directly to delivery zone" flow.
- Difficulty presets now exist in Resources and can be discovered without manual inspector wiring.
- The campaign win state is treated as a closed campaign, like game over, for orders/shop/day flow.
- Optional order parts are respected by the main order UI, crafting logic, and compact recipe displays.
- Saving is file-based, not PlayerPrefs-based.


9. RECOMMENDED UNITY VALIDATION AFTER CHANGES
---------------------------------------------

- Verify the new difficulty preset assets appear in the project
- Enter Play Mode
- Confirm:
  - orders stop generating after win
  - shop trading stops after win
  - day progression stops after win
  - forest ground/trees/range build correctly
  - optional-part orders display correctly on the blueprint display
  - Easy / Normal / Hard presets load correctly if selected
